# Phishing Incident Investigation

## Project Overview

This project involved investigating a phishing-related security alert triggered by a malicious email attachment delivered to an employee.

The objective was to analyze phishing indicators, validate the threat using previously identified malware intelligence, document findings, assess business impact, and determine the appropriate incident response actions in accordance with Security Operations Center (SOC) procedures.

---

## Objective

The purpose of this project was to:

- Investigate a phishing email reported by security monitoring systems
- Analyze email-based indicators of compromise
- Validate malicious artifacts using threat intelligence
- Assess alert severity and potential business impact
- Document investigation findings
- Follow incident response and escalation procedures

---

## Key Activities

### Email Investigation

- Investigated a phishing email reported by security monitoring systems
- Reviewed email content and attachment characteristics
- Examined suspicious communication indicators

### Email Analysis

- Analyzed email headers and sender information
- Reviewed message content for social engineering tactics
- Evaluated inconsistencies between sender identity and email content
- Identified phishing-related characteristics

### Threat Validation

- Verified a known malicious SHA-256 file hash associated with the attachment
- Compared indicators against existing threat intelligence information
- Confirmed malicious characteristics of the email attachment

### Incident Response

- Assessed alert severity and business impact
- Documented investigation findings in an incident ticket
- Escalated the incident according to SOC procedures and playbook guidance
- Supported response activities through structured documentation

---

## Indicators Identified

The investigation identified several phishing-related indicators:

- Suspicious sender address
- Mismatch between sender identity and email content
- Password-protected executable attachment
- Known malicious SHA-256 file hash
- Grammar and formatting anomalies commonly associated with phishing campaigns

---

## Key Findings

### Social Engineering Indicators

The email demonstrated several characteristics commonly associated with phishing attacks, including:

- Deceptive sender information
- Suspicious messaging techniques
- Attempts to create trust or urgency
- Inconsistent communication patterns

### Malicious Attachment

The attachment was identified as suspicious due to:

- Password protection used to bypass email security controls
- Association with a known malicious SHA-256 hash
- Potential malware delivery capability

### Business Risk

The phishing email represented a significant risk because successful execution of the attachment could have resulted in:

- Malware infection
- Credential theft
- Unauthorized access
- Operational disruption
- Data compromise

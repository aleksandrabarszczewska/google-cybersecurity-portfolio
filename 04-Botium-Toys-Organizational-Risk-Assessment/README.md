# Botium Toys – Organizational Risk Assessment

## Overview

This project presents a governance-focused cybersecurity risk assessment conducted for Botium Toys, a fictional retail organization experiencing rapid business growth and expanding e-commerce operations.

The assessment evaluated operational, technical, and compliance-related risks affecting the organization's security posture and aligned findings with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF).

## Business Context

Botium Toys operates:

- Physical retail locations
- Warehouse facilities
- E-commerce platforms
- Internal IT systems managing customer and payment information

Rapid growth increased exposure to operational, compliance, and cybersecurity risks requiring formal assessment and mitigation planning.

## Assessment Focus Areas

The risk assessment focused on:

- Asset Management
- Data Protection
- Access Governance
- Business Continuity
- Regulatory Compliance
- Security Governance

## Risks Identified

### Governance Risks

- Lack of formalized security governance processes
- Incomplete asset classification and ownership
- Weak access governance practices

### Operational Risks

- Missing disaster recovery planning
- Inadequate backup procedures
- Dependence on legacy systems

### Compliance Risks

- Potential GDPR violations
- PCI DSS compliance gaps
- Insufficient protection of sensitive customer data

### Security Risks

- Missing intrusion detection capabilities (IDS)
- Weak password management practices
- Excessive employee access permissions

## Governance Perspective

The assessment emphasized:

- Risk-based decision making
- Security governance maturity
- Regulatory alignment
- Business continuity planning
- Organizational accountability
- Stakeholder-focused remediation strategies
